## A New Era of Building Regulations

Building codes have traditionally focused on prescriptive requirements: use this thickness of insulation, install this type of window, include this amount of ventilation. Building performance standards take a fundamentally different approach. They set targets for actual energy consumption or carbon emissions and allow builders and homeowners to choose how to meet those targets.

This shift is accelerating across North America and Europe, and it has significant implications for anyone planning to build or renovate a home.

## What Are Building Performance Standards?

Building Performance Standards, often abbreviated as BPS, are regulations that require buildings to meet specific energy or emissions benchmarks. Unlike traditional codes that prescribe specific materials and methods, BPS focus on measurable outcomes.

Key characteristics include:

- **Outcome-based targets** measured in energy use intensity or carbon emissions per square foot
- **Compliance timelines** that give building owners deadlines to meet performance thresholds
- **Reporting requirements** that mandate regular energy use disclosure
- **Escalating standards** that tighten over time, pushing buildings toward net-zero

### Where Are They Being Adopted?

As of early 2026, more than 40 cities and several states have adopted or proposed building performance standards. Major jurisdictions include New York City, Washington DC, Boston, Denver, and the state of Colorado. Canada's National Energy Code and the EU's Energy Performance of Buildings Directive are driving similar requirements internationally.

While many current BPS target large commercial buildings, residential standards are expanding. Several jurisdictions now include multifamily buildings, and single-family requirements are on the horizon in progressive markets.

## How This Affects Homeowners

### New Construction

If you are building a new home, performance standards increasingly influence what your builder must deliver. In many jurisdictions, new homes must now meet energy efficiency levels that would have been considered exceptional just five years ago. This includes:

- Tighter air sealing requirements, often below 3 ACH50
- Higher insulation minimums across all climate zones
- Mandatory mechanical ventilation with heat recovery in many areas
- Electric-ready or all-electric requirements in some jurisdictions

### Existing Homes and Renovations

For existing homeowners, the impact is most immediately felt during major renovations. Many jurisdictions now require that significant renovations trigger compliance with current energy codes, which may mean upgrading insulation, windows, and mechanical systems beyond what was originally planned.

Looking further ahead, some BPS programs will begin requiring existing homes to meet performance benchmarks at time of sale or by specific compliance dates. Understanding these requirements early allows homeowners to plan upgrades strategically rather than reacting to last-minute mandates.

## Staying Ahead of the Curve

### Get an Energy Assessment

Understanding your home's current performance is the essential first step. A professional energy assessment provides a baseline and identifies the most impactful upgrades. This data is valuable whether you are planning renovations now or preparing for future compliance requirements.

### Plan Upgrades Strategically

Rather than waiting for mandates, consider incorporating performance upgrades into planned maintenance and renovation projects. Replacing a roof is an opportunity to add insulation. Updating HVAC equipment is a chance to right-size for a tighter envelope. This staged approach spreads costs over time and avoids the premium of rushed compliance work.

### Document Everything

As performance reporting becomes more common, maintaining records of energy upgrades, equipment specifications, and energy audit results will be increasingly valuable. This documentation supports compliance, improves resale value, and helps future owners understand the home's performance characteristics.

### Connect with Knowledgeable Professionals

Not all builders, architects, and contractors are equally familiar with performance-based standards. Seeking out professionals who understand building science and have experience with high-performance construction is essential. ## The Opportunity in Compliance

While new regulations can feel burdensome, building performance standards are ultimately driving the market toward better homes. Homes that meet or exceed these standards are more comfortable, less expensive to operate, more resilient to extreme weather, and increasingly valued by buyers. Homeowners who invest in performance now are not just meeting requirements but are making their homes better places to live.
